Every evergreen browser as of 2023 — Chrome, Edge, Safari, and Firefox 121+ all support :has().

grid-template-rows animates from 0fr to 1fr. Grid's fr unit can be transitioned and doesn't require knowing the content's height ahead of time, unlike animating max-height.

<details> works great too, but its open/close transition support is newer and less consistent. A checkbox + :has() gives full control over the closed→open animation with plain CSS.

Zero JavaScript. Each row is a hidden checkbox + label; the parent .item:has(input:checked) selector reaches "up" from the checkbox to react on its own container — the trick that made CSS-only tabs/accordions/dropdowns finally practical.
